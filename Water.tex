\begin{abstract}
\noindent The last two decades have been full of improvements to water quality and accessibility throughout the developing world.
The next phase of improvements must now focus on harder to reach groups and will require the use of large quantities of data and careful data analysis.
This article outlines a modeling framework to utilize prevalent national survey data to advise in national and local decision making using a qualitative cost-benefit approach.
Roughly based on the cost-benefit analysis of the World Health Organization, this model uses statistical distributions and geometric inference to fill data gaps and give effective interventions.
It also includes a multi-stage model for budget efficiency and advanced planning.
\end{abstract}
